ISOLA

General

In this turn-based game your goal is to trap your opponents on a single field and be the last person that can still move.
To take part in a game, you have to join a room that still has empty spaces. The game starts when enough players have entered the room. There are two-player and four-player rooms to choose from.
To distinguish the different players, they are marked with colors[image]. The player whose turn it is has a stronger color that the others.

Movement

When your turn starts, you first have to move. You can move to any unoccupied adjacent field, both straight and diagonal. The green Marker shows you wether a field is valid or not. [clip]
After you moved your character, you need to remove one field. This can be any unoccupied field across the whole board. The blue marker shows you wether a field is valid or not. [clip]
As soon as you removed a field, your turn is over and the next player has to make his/her move.

Winning Conditions

To win, you need to be the last person who is still able to move. If there are two players (in two-player-mode) trapped on three connected fields, the player who removes the unoccupied field wins, since he was the last one to finish a complete turn. [image/clip]

Rooms

Only ten rooms can exist at the same time. Therefore, if there is no action inside a room for more than five minutes, the room will be shut down to make space for more active rooms. Actions that reset this timer are: a new player joining the room, moving to a field, and removin a field.
If you press the "New Game"-button, you can create a new room. You can decide the following settings: Number of total players (2 or 4), number of computer opponents, and the starting position of the computer opponents. [images]
